How to cite the Decision Provenance Standard under CC-BY 4.0.

Citation guidance, not legal advice. This page documents the recommended citation format and the attribution string for the Decision Provenance Standard under CC-BY 4.0. Citation guidance does not constitute legal advice on permissible reuse in any jurisdiction. A citation that conforms with the format below may not satisfy the license terms in every reuse context, and a deployer's specific reuse questions should be reviewed by counsel in the relevant jurisdiction.

Jurisdiction Assumed: U.S. federal and Delaware as primary; UK, EU AI Act, and Israel as named secondaries. Whether a Member-State implementation of the EU Copyright Directive treats a specific reuse as joint-authorship or derivative-work creation is a question only counsel in that Member State can answer.

This UPL block requires General Counsel sign-off before production publish.

The Decision Provenance Standard is published under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC-BY 4.0). Reuse, quotation, adaptation, and redistribution are encouraged under the license's attribution terms.

Citations to the Standard structurally conform with CC-BY 4.0 attribution requirements when they include author, title, version, URL, and license. Citations that omit any of these elements still align with the Standard's open-publication intent but may not satisfy the license terms.

Recommended citation formats

APA (7th edition)

Etsion, Y. (2026). The Decision Provenance Standard (Version 1.0). Etsion Brands Ltd. https://decisionprovenance.ai/read

Chicago (Author-Date)

Etsion, Yohay. 2026. "The Decision Provenance Standard." Version 1.0. Etsion Brands Ltd. https://decisionprovenance.ai/read.

BibTeX

@misc{etsion2026dps,
  author       = {Etsion, Yohay},
  title        = {The Decision Provenance Standard},
  version      = {1.0},
  year         = {2026},
  publisher    = {Etsion Brands Ltd.},
  howpublished = {\url{https://decisionprovenance.ai/read}},
  note         = {CC-BY 4.0}
}

Canonical URL pattern

The Standard is citable by section using stable URL fragments. The canonical pattern is:

https://decisionprovenance.ai/read#section-N
https://decisionprovenance.ai/read#section-N-M
https://decisionprovenance.ai/read#section-N-M-P

Where N is the top-level section number (1 through 10), M is the sub-section, and P is the sub-sub-clause where one is named in the Standard.

Path B note — if the Standard hub folds into the Series hub, the canonical URL pattern updates to https://executiveoperatingsystems.org/standard/read#section-N. Citations to v1.0 made before the Path A/B decision retain their original URLs; we will not break inbound citation links.

Attribution string

For reuse contexts where a full citation is impractical (slide decks, poster sessions, social media), the minimum attribution string is:

"The Decision Provenance Standard" v1.0 by Yohay Etsion / Etsion Brands Ltd., licensed under CC-BY 4.0. https://decisionprovenance.ai/read

Reuse that adapts, modifies, or derives from the Standard MUST also indicate the changes made per the CC-BY 4.0 ShareAlike-adjacent attribution requirements.

When the Standard updates

The Standard follows semantic versioning. v1.0 is the inaugural version. Future versions (v1.1, v2.0, …) will preserve cite-able URLs for all prior versions; cite the version you read by including the version number in the citation.

To cite a specific past version, append the version to the URL: https://decisionprovenance.ai/read?v=1.0. Versioned URLs return the version frozen as of its publication date.
