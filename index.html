Coming from somewhere else?

The Decision Provenance Standard is the open foundation document for the Executive Operating Systems series — the artifact set, the dispatch grammar, the conformance levels. CC-BY 4.0. No certification track. No certifying body. If you came here directly, start with the full Standard →.

If you arrived from another artifact and want orientation first: from a CIO referral, the Position Paper is the category framing that explains why an open standard rather than a certified product. From Vision to Value, this is the foundation document the book follows. From a search on AI governance, the Position Paper introduces the category, then the Standard formalizes the artifact set. The Standard does not opine on whether your specific deployment satisfies any particular regulatory regime; that is your counsel's substantive work, with the Standard as one input among others.

The Decision Provenance Standard formalizes a single artifact set and a single dispatch grammar under which a deployer commits to a way of deciding that survives leadership transitions and supports counsel and auditors when they prepare evidence, certifications, or attestations.

The Standard is open. There is no certification track. There is no certifying body.

The Standard structurally conforms with the open-publication discipline that lets deployers, counsel, auditors, and regulators read it freely and implement it without licensing.

What this Standard claims — and what it does not claim

  • The Standard formalizes an artifact set and a dispatch grammar. It is not evidence.
  • The Standard grades a Charter against three Conformance Levels. It is not a certification.
  • The Standard provides inputs to attestation work. It is not an attestation.
  • The Standard does not opine on regulatory acceptance. It is not a regulatory substitute.
